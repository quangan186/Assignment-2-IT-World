  • CLO1: describe and apply basic concepts of contemporary technologies

Ready for Life and Work

You will develop the skills to critically assess a contemporary and practical IT topic and apply appropriate IT knowledge to address the issues related to that topic.  Professionals possessing these skills can effectively contribute to the success of their IT career and/or organisations through identifying opportunities for improvements and proactively making practical changes to improve the current situation.

3. Project Ideas

You are to write around 1000 words describing your project idea. You should describe the project itself, as well as why you think it is important or interesting, and any potential drawbacks or obstacles you anticipate. What are you passionate about? What kind of IT innovation would you like to see? What project will help you develop the skills, experience, and knowledge required to make you qualified for your ideal job?

You need not have full answers to any of these questions, but you are required to have some ideas about an IT project that you think will address at least one of these questions. It may not be possible to find one that answers all three, but it is quite likely that something you would find interesting or useful will have a similar effect on others.

There are a number of possible types of projects that you may consider. Some potential ideas are below; others will be discussed in class.

  • Developing a game using tools such as Unity, Construct2 or Cocos2d.
  • Developing a smartphone app, using whatever programming language in which you have the expertise, or by using tools such as MIT App Inventor.
  • Finding an innovative application for new hardware developments, such as Raspberry Pis, Arduinos, Makey Makeys, small robots or related technologies.
  • Creating a FAQ and implementing it as a chatterbot, possibly involving speech-to-text and text-to-speech software.
  • Building an electronic artefact such as a model for Minecraft or similar games, along the lines of the United Nations Block by Block project.

This section should not only describe what the project would be, but also why you think it is important or interesting, and what potential drawbacks or obstacles you anticipate. Your description should include the following sections. The word counts below are recommendations only.

  • Overview (100 words) This should be a summary of what the project will be.
  • Motivation (100) words) This should be a description of why the project will be interesting or useful. This may include statistics or other evidence, such as "There are 1.5 billion cat owners in Australia, and so there is a huge market for an automated cat feeder. Using Raspberry Pi technology is a cheap and easily assembled solution to this problem.''
  • Description (500 words) Detailed description of the features of the product or service.
  • Tools and Technologies (100 words) Describe the software, hardware and/or other equipment needed. Include any relevant open source tools as appropriate.
  • Skills Required (100 words) List the skills are required for your project, including software that needs to be written, and special hardware (if any). How feasible will it be to find the skills, software, and hardware required?
  • Outcome (100 words) If the project is successful, what will be the outcome? How will the original problem be solved? What impact will this development have?

4. Feedback

A key feature of working in the industry is the ability to give and receive feedback. In order to give you some experience at this, you will get at least two (or more if possible) other students in class to give you some feedback on your project idea.

You will reflect on and briefly discuss this feedback in about 300 words. Some ideas for your discussion include: how useful and constructive is the feedback? Do you agree or disagree with the feedback, and why? How you will use the feedback for future improvements?

 

5. IT Technologies

There are a lot of fascinating developments going on in the IT world, many of which may fall by the wayside, but some of which are likely to change the way the world works. Historic examples of such developments include the Internet, smartphones, cloud computing and public-key cryptography.

In the previous section, you have described and explained why your project idea is interesting and significant. In this section, you will discuss in more detail the technologies/areas that are relevant to your project idea. Some of these relevant technologies/areas can be found in the list below - you need to discuss at least two areas:

  • Cloud computing (services, platforms, servers etc.)
  • Cybersecurity and privacy
  • Blockchain and cryptocurrencies
  • Artificial intelligence and machine learning
  • Autonomous vehicles
  • Natural Language processing and chatterbots
  • Robots
  • Raspberry Pis, Arduinos, Makey Makeys, and other small computing devices
  • etc.

 

For each of the areas covered, you should report on the following. Please note, the word limits are recommendations only.

What does it do? (600 words) ​What is the state of the art of this new technology? What can be done now? What is likely to be able to do be done soon (say in the next 3 years)? What technological or other developments make this possible?

What is the likely impact? (300 words) ​What is the potential impact of this development? What is likely to change? Which people will be most affected and how? Will this create, replace or make redundant any current jobs or technologies?

How will this affect you? (300 words) ​In your daily life, how will this affect you? What will be different for you? How might this affect members of your family or your friends?

 

6. IT Work

After completing the described project, you should be able to develop some new knowledge and skills that contribute to your future desirable career(s). In this section, you will gather information to describe the daily work of your future desirable job. There are two ways in which you can do this, as below.

  1. Interview an IT professional; OR
  2. View, summarise and discuss at least 5 and maximum 10 YouTube videos or other web sources (you may be surprised how many hits there are for a Google search on ​A day in the life of an IT professional).

You need to choose one of these two approaches.

If it all possible, it is strongly recommended that you interview an IT professional. Not only will this information be more direct, but it may also provide you with a contact that you will find helpful in the future. If you choose this option, you will receive some basic training on how to go about this interview.

If you choose the web sources option, you need to report on 5 different IT professionals, as well as indicate your 10 sources.

Whichever source of information you use, you should answer the following questions.

  1. What kind of work is done by the IT professional?
  2. What kinds of people does the IT professional interact with? Are they other IT professionals? Clients? Investors? The general public?
  3. Where do the IT professionals spend most of their time?
  4. What aspect of their position is most challenging?
